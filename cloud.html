Frequently Asked Questions

Will the Aristotle Metadata Registry still be open-source?

Absolutely - now and for ever

The Aristotle Metadata Registry is open-source through and through. We designed Aristotle to be an open-source product, we use a open-source frameworks like Django, Bootstrap and Haystack, and the basis of our product is the open ISO/IEC 11179 metadata standard.

Aristotle-Cloud is designed as an option for organisations who are as committed to open-source as we are but don't have the in-house capacity to manage or deploy a solution themselves. What Aristotle-Cloud will provide is an opportunity clients to outsource management of their metadata registry to our turn-key, enterpise-grade, {% comment %}farm-to-table, {% endcomment %} cloud-based server farm so they can get on with the business of data management. Users will have as much control over their registry as if they installed it themselves, without the overhead of infrastructure and software management.

Where is my cloud host registry hosted?

Our primary cloud cluster is hosted within the United States, however our infrastructure supports a managed installation in a variety of locations around the world, including various countries across Europe, North America and the Asia-Pacific.

To access your choice of location can require additional setup and may incur additional costs for setup and ongoing maintainance.

Can I use my own domain with Aristotle-Cloud?

Yes — as long as you have a the domain registered and are allowed to use it, we can point your registry to any domain you need. If you are looking to host on a .gov domain (or .gov.uk, .gov.au, etc...) you'll need to register that through your government domain provider.

For all other publically accessible domains (.com, .co.uk, etc...) we can help manage domain registration on your behalf.

Once you have a domain, we can setup DNS records to point your cloud in the right direction.

Can I theme Aristotle to match a our existing website style?

Absolutely, your Aristotle Metadata Registry can be themed to match your corporate style, and we can work with you to make sure your registry looks just that - yours!

Depending on complexity of the design there may be additional setup costs to ensure your site can be smoothly updated as future releases of the platform become available.

Once I hit the limit of teams or users for my plan, can I get more?

Only active users or teams count against your limits so you can always deactivate users who no longer use the system, or archive teams to increase capacity.

If you find that your needs are growing beyond what your current plan supports we suggest upgrading to a higher plan to ensure that your registry is able to support your organisations needs.

What are community and enterprise plugins?

Community plugins are the free open-source extensions managed by the Aristotle Metadata Community. These cover additional download formats, new metadata types, administration themes and other assorted plug-ins.

Enterprise plugins are specialty extensions and plugins that are only provided for Enterprise users. {% comment %} or members of the Aristotle Enterprise Community {% endcomment %} These extensions are of an enterprise specific nature, such as support for single sign-on, enhanced user security, IP restrictions or integration with enterprise software.

Are there setup costs?

No — there aren't any extra costs associated with setting up your cloud metadata registry. Once you choose your configuration and we setup all the financials, setting up a new registry is a snap.

However, if you have large volumes of metadata to migrate or have special design requirements, we can discuss consulting options with you when you sign up.

What are Whoosh and Amazon ElasticSearch?

Whoosh is an Open-Source Python-powered search rock solid search engine that has powered Aristotle from almost the first line of code. Its quick for users and quick to set-up and is a perfect low cost search engine for smaller users.

Amazon Elasticsearch is the enterprise search engine provided by the Amazon Web Services. Its highly scalable and engineered for performance. As more and more metadata is stored in your registry, we configure ElasticSearch to scale up with it to ensure search is always quick and reliable.

I have a feature request, how can I get it included in Aristotle?

Because we are open-source, we are always accepting bugs and feature requests, but we prioritise based on community interest, and time retraints. If you have programming experience, you are more than welcome to join our community and help us build a better product for everyone to use.

If you have a special requirement and aren't in a position to develop it yourself, we take requests and complete them as time permits. But if you want to sponsor a new feature or extension our consulting page has all the details for contacting us to implement it at an accelerated pace.
